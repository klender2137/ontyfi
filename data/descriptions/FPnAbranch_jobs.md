# Financial Planning & Analysis (FP&A) Careers

## Introduction

Financial Planning & Analysis teams influence decisions behind **billions of dollars in corporate spending every year**. Large companies often run **multi-billion dollar operating budgets**, and FP&A teams monitor thousands of financial data points across departments. In the U.S. alone, corporate finance departments employ **hundreds of thousands of analysts and managers**, making FP&A one of the most common entry points into strategic finance.

The team analyzes operational data, builds financial models, and explains performance gaps between what a company expected and what actually happened. In simple terms, FP&A translates company activity into numbers and turns those numbers into decisions.

---

# Entry Jobs in FP&A

Below are five common roles across the career ladder, starting from the first entry job.

---

## 1. FP&A Analyst

**Typical remuneration:** $65k – $90k  
**Working hours:** ~45–55 hours per week  
**Relative difficulty to obtain:** 2 / 5  

The analyst is responsible for the operational financial analysis work that keeps the planning process accurate. Most early career professionals in corporate finance start here.

**Example tasks**

- Compare monthly actual spending with the approved budget and produce variance explanations for management.
- Update the rolling financial forecast model using the most recent operational data.
- Build a unit economics analysis calculating metrics such as LTV/CAC or product margin.

---

## 2. Senior FP&A Analyst

**Typical remuneration:** $85k – $120k  
**Working hours:** ~45–55 hours per week  
**Relative difficulty to obtain:** 3 / 5  

Senior analysts move beyond reporting and start influencing decisions by interpreting data and improving financial models.

**Example tasks**

- Build scenario models evaluating revenue changes and cost adjustments on cash runway.
- Analyze working capital indicators such as DSO to improve company cash flow.
- Support department heads in building operational cost projections for their teams.

---

## 3. FP&A Manager

**Typical remuneration:** $110k – $150k  
**Working hours:** ~50–60 hours per week  
**Relative difficulty to obtain:** 4 / 5  

Managers coordinate the planning process and act as financial partners to business units.

**Example tasks**

- Lead the annual bottom-up budgeting process with department leaders.
- Design financial models evaluating compensation plans or operational initiatives.
- Present financial insights to executives explaining performance gaps and risk areas.

---

## 4. Director of FP&A

**Typical remuneration:** $150k – $220k  
**Working hours:** ~55–65 hours per week  
**Relative difficulty to obtain:** 5 / 5  

Directors focus on strategic financial planning and capital allocation decisions.

**Example tasks**

- Convert detailed financial results into board-level performance narratives.
- Evaluate whether acquisitions delivered the expected financial synergies.
- Manage lender relationships and ensure compliance with debt covenants.

---

## 5. VP of FP&A

**Typical remuneration:** $220k – $350k+  
**Working hours:** ~60+ hours per week  
**Relative difficulty to obtain:** 5 / 5  

The VP leads corporate financial strategy and works directly with the CEO and CFO.

**Example tasks**

- Design the long-range financial strategy covering the next 3–5 years.
- Prepare investor relations materials and earnings call messaging.
- Evaluate large capital allocation decisions such as expansions or acquisitions.

# Institutional Context: Where FP&A Exists

FP&A functions exist across many types of financial institutions and companies. Below are five major organizational environments and how FP&A roles differ within them.

---

## 1. Large Public Corporations

**FP&A Analyst**

Works mainly on reporting, variance tracking, and financial consolidation across multiple divisions.

**FP&A Manager**

Coordinates budgets across departments and ensures alignment with corporate targets.

**FP&A Director**

Focuses on strategic planning, earnings preparation, and communication with the board.

---

## 2. Technology Companies / Startups

**FP&A Analyst**

Tracks product metrics, customer acquisition costs, and revenue growth indicators.

**FP&A Manager**

Runs scenario modeling for funding rounds, hiring expansion, and product launches.

**FP&A Director**

Designs financial strategy for scaling the business while managing burn rate and cash runway.

---

## 3. Private Equity Portfolio Companies

**FP&A Analyst**

Builds operating dashboards tracking margins, revenue growth, and efficiency metrics.

**FP&A Manager**

Prepares financial reports required by private equity owners and lenders.

**FP&A Director**

Drives profitability improvement initiatives and supports exit planning (sale or IPO).

---

## 4. Industrial / Manufacturing Firms

**FP&A Analyst**

Analyzes production costs, inventory levels, and operational spending.

**FP&A Manager**

Optimizes working capital including inventory turnover and supplier payment terms.

**FP&A Director**

Evaluates plant investments and large capital expenditures.

---

## 5. Financial Institutions (Banks, Insurance)

**FP&A Analyst**

Monitors financial product profitability and regulatory cost reporting.

**FP&A Manager**

Builds forecasts based on interest rates, lending activity, and market trends.

**FP&A Director**

Advises executives on balance sheet strategy and long-term financial positioning.

---

# Interview Topics for FP&A Roles

FP&A interviews typically focus on a mix of financial theory and practical analytics knowledge.

Three common **finance theory topics** appear frequently. Candidates are often asked to explain **variance analysis**, including how to interpret differences between actual results and forecasts. Another frequent topic is **working capital management**, particularly metrics such as Days Sales Outstanding and their impact on company liquidity. Interviewers also test understanding of **financial modeling concepts** like discounted cash flow or scenario forecasting.

Two **technical skills** are also regularly tested. Excel proficiency is essential, with candidates expected to demonstrate advanced functions, financial models, and sensitivity analysis. Data visualization and analytics tools such as Power BI, Tableau, or SQL are increasingly important for building automated reporting dashboards.

Strong candidates show both analytical thinking and the ability to explain financial insights clearly to non-finance stakeholders.

---

# Conclusion

FP&A sits at the center of corporate decision making because every strategic choice eventually translates into financial numbers. The career path offers a gradual progression from analytical modeling roles toward strategic advisory positions working directly with executives. Students who enjoy combining data analysis, business understanding, and long-term planning often find FP&A particularly attractive because it allows them to see how financial insights directly shape company strategy.
