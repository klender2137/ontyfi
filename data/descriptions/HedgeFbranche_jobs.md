# Hedge Funds: Careers, Roles

## Introduction

Hedge funds manage **over $4 trillion in global assets**, operating across equities, derivatives, commodities, and complex financial instruments. Top portfolio managers can earn **$10 million or more annually**, while even junior professionals often start with **six-figure compensation packages**. Despite employing far fewer people than banks, hedge funds influence **billions of dollars in daily trading activity** and major market trends.

## 1. Junior Analyst / Research Associate

**Typical entry role for graduates joining directly from university.**

**Compensation:** $100K – $150K  
**Hours:** 60-70 hours per week  
**Difficulty:** 3 / 5

**Example tasks**

- Build basic financial models and update valuation spreadsheets for companies tracked by the team.  
- Gather industry data, earnings transcripts, and regulatory filings to support research projects.  
- Monitor news and market movements affecting existing portfolio positions.

---

## 2. Hedge Fund Analyst

**Core investing role responsible for developing investment ideas.**

**Compensation:** $200K – $600K  
**Hours:** 60-70 hours per week  
**Difficulty:** 4 / 5

**Example tasks**

- Build detailed financial models to estimate intrinsic value and scenario outcomes for companies.  
- Conduct calls with suppliers, customers, and management teams to validate investment theses.  
- Present investment recommendations and defend them during portfolio review meetings.

---

## 3. Quantitative Researcher

**Specialized role focused on systematic trading strategies and statistical models.**

**Compensation:** $200K – $500K  
**Hours:** 55-65 hours per week  
**Difficulty:** 5 / 5

**Example tasks**

- Develop statistical trading models using historical price and alternative datasets.  
- Test strategy performance through large-scale backtests across market regimes.  
- Optimize algorithms that generate trade signals or portfolio allocations.

---

## 4. Execution Trader

**Responsible for executing trades efficiently once investment decisions are made.**

**Compensation:** $150K – $200K entry; up to $500K senior  
**Hours:** 55-65 hours per week  
**Difficulty:** 3 / 5

**Example tasks**

- Break large trade orders into smaller blocks to minimize market impact.  
- Monitor liquidity and order book dynamics while executing portfolio manager instructions.  
- Coordinate with brokers and electronic trading platforms to secure optimal pricing.

---

## 5. Data / Research Engineer

**Technical role supporting research teams through infrastructure and data pipelines.**

**Compensation:** $120K – $220K  
**Hours:** 50-60 hours per week  
**Difficulty:** 3 / 5

**Example tasks**

- Build automated pipelines that collect and clean financial and alternative datasets.  
- Maintain databases used for financial modeling and trading research.  
- Deploy analytical tools used by analysts and quantitative researchers.

# Hedge Fund Types and Role Differences

Different hedge fund strategies change **how analysts, traders, and portfolio managers operate**. Below are five major fund types and how the three key roles differ inside them.

---

## 1. Long / Short Equity Funds

**Analyst**

- Builds fundamental company models to identify undervalued long positions and overvalued short positions.  
- Conducts sector research and channel checks to validate earnings forecasts.  
- Tracks catalysts such as earnings surprises or industry disruptions.

**Trader**

- Executes stock purchases and short sales while managing borrow availability for short positions.  
- Adjusts order size depending on market liquidity.  
- Maintains efficient entry and exit for active trading strategies.

**Portfolio Manager**

- Allocates capital between long and short positions while controlling net exposure.  
- Balances sector concentration and diversification across the portfolio.  
- Adjusts positioning based on macro conditions and market sentiment.

---

## 2. Global Macro Funds

**Analyst**

- Researches macroeconomic variables such as interest rates, inflation, and currency flows.  
- Builds models linking macro indicators to asset price movements.  
- Monitors central bank policy and geopolitical developments.

**Trader**

- Executes trades across futures, currencies, and interest rate derivatives.  
- Manages rapid position changes when macro news moves markets.  
- Tracks liquidity across global exchanges.

**Portfolio Manager**

- Designs top-down trades across asset classes based on macro trends.  
- Manages leverage and cross-asset hedging strategies.  
- Repositions the portfolio when macro regimes shift.

---

## 3. Event-Driven Funds

**Analyst**

- Analyzes mergers, acquisitions, restructurings, and bankruptcies.  
- Builds probability models estimating deal completion outcomes.  
- Tracks regulatory approvals and shareholder votes.

**Trader**

- Executes trades in equities and derivatives tied to corporate events.  
- Adjusts exposure when new deal information emerges.  
- Maintains positions across complex arbitrage structures.

**Portfolio Manager**

- Allocates capital across multiple event opportunities.  
- Evaluates deal risk relative to expected spread returns.  
- Manages portfolio exposure to event timing and legal risk.

---

## 4. Quantitative / Systematic Funds

**Analyst / Quant Researcher**

- Develops algorithmic strategies using statistical and machine learning techniques.  
- Tests signals across historical datasets and market conditions.  
- Refines models to reduce overfitting and improve robustness.

**Trader**

- Monitors automated trading systems and algorithm execution.  
- Ensures trades follow model instructions and liquidity constraints.  
- Adjusts algorithms if market conditions disrupt execution.

**Portfolio Manager**

- Oversees strategy allocation and capital distribution across models.  
- Controls portfolio risk and correlation between strategies.  
- Adjusts parameters when strategy performance changes.

---

## 5. Credit / Distressed Debt Funds

**Analyst**

- Evaluates corporate balance sheets and debt structures.  
- Models recovery values in bankruptcy scenarios.  
- Researches legal and restructuring processes affecting creditors.

**Trader**

- Executes trades in bonds, loans, and credit derivatives.  
- Tracks liquidity differences between credit instruments.  
- Adjusts positions when restructuring negotiations evolve.

**Portfolio Manager**

- Allocates capital to distressed or mispriced credit opportunities.  
- Manages downside risk tied to default probability.  
- Coordinates strategy around restructuring timelines.

# Hedge Fund Interview Topics

Recruiting interviews often test **theoretical finance knowledge and technical analytical skills**.

Three theory topics appear frequently. First is **valuation methodology**, especially discounted cash flow modeling and comparable company analysis. Second is **portfolio risk management**, including concepts like diversification, correlation, and exposure limits. Third is **market efficiency and behavioral finance**, which interviewers use to evaluate how candidates think about market mispricing.

Two technical areas also appear often. Candidates are frequently tested on **financial modeling and Excel skills**, including building valuation models quickly. Programming skills are increasingly required as well, especially **Python, data analysis, and statistical testing** for research roles.

Interviewers may also ask candidates to **pitch a stock or investment idea**, evaluating analytical thinking and market awareness.

---

# Summary

Hedge funds represent one of the most intellectually demanding segments of finance, combining research, trading, and portfolio construction in a highly competitive environment. Careers typically start with analyst or research roles before progressing toward portfolio management, where responsibility and compensation increase dramatically. The sector attracts people who enjoy **deep analysis, fast decision-making, and constant exposure to global economic forces**.
