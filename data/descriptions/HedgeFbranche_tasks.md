# Tasks

**ID:** tasks
**Branch:** Hedge Funds

## Description


## 1. Constructing Three-Statement Financial Models
Analysts utilize **Microsoft Excel** to build integrated financial models consisting of the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement. The objective is to project future earnings and free cash flow under various operating scenarios.

## 2. Spreading Trading Comparables and Relative Valuation
Using data from **Bloomberg** or **FactSet**, analysts maintain a database of valuation multiples, such as EV/EBITDA, P/E, and Price-to-Book, for a peer group of companies. The objective is to determine a company's relative value within its sector. These "comp sheets" allow Portfolio Managers to identify "long" opportunities in undervalued assets or "short" candidates in overvalued ones.

## 3. Monitoring Real-Time Factor Exposures
Analysts use risk management software such as **MSCI Barra** or **AxiomSL** to track the portfolio's sensitivity to macroeconomic factors like interest rates, volatility (VIX), and momentum. The objective is to ensure the fund remains within its mandated risk limits.  

## 4. Conducting Channel Checks and Primary Research
Juniors perform primary research by interviewing industry consultants, suppliers, and customers to verify company-reported data. The objective is to gather "boots-on-the-ground" intelligence regarding product demand or supply chain disruptions. The findings are compiled into internal research notes used by senior investors to validate or challenge an investment thesis.

## 5. Analyzing Alternative Data Sets
Analysts utilize **Python** or **SQL** to process non-traditional data, such as satellite imagery, credit card transaction flows, or web-scraped pricing. The objective is to gain an information advantage over the broader market.  

## 6. Performing Event-Driven Trade Analysis
Using **Microsoft Excel**, analysts model the potential outcomes of corporate actions such as mergers, spin-offs, or bankruptcies. The objective is to calculate the "spread" between the current price and the expected closing price.  



## 7. Generating Daily P&L and Attribution Reports
Analysts pull trade data from the fund’s **Order Management System (OMS)** to attribute daily gains or losses to specific positions, sectors, or market movements. The objective is to identify which bets are driving performance.  

## 8. Managing the Short-Interest and Borrow Database
Juniors coordinate with prime brokers via platforms like **Goldman Sachs 360** or **Morgan Stanley Matrix** to check the availability and cost of "borrows" for short positions. The objective is to secure the necessary shares for executing short sales. 

## 9. Preparing Investment Committee Memos
Analysts synthesize their research into concise written documents that outline the investment thesis, key risks, and expected catalysts. The objective is to provide a structured format for evaluating a trade. 

## 10. Executing Post-Trade Compliance Checks
Using compliance modules within the fund's **Execution Management System (EMS)**, analysts verify that all executed trades comply with the fund's offering memorandum and regulatory rules (e.g., UCITS or SEC limits).

# Strategic Tasks of Senior Hedge Fund Professionals

## 1. Capital Allocation and Risk Budgeting
Portfolio Managers and Managing Directors determine the high-level distribution of the fund's capital across different strategies, asset classes, and geographies. This involves setting "stop-loss" limits and deciding when to "size up" on high-conviction ideas. This strategy ensures the fund's overall volatility remains consistent with its long-term objectives and investor expectations.

## 2. Institutional Fundraising and Investor Relations
Partners and Directors lead the process of raising capital from institutional investors such as pension funds, endowments, and sovereign wealth funds. They present the fund’s performance history, strategy, and risk management framework to secure "sticky" long-term capital. Success in this area determines the fund's total Assets Under Management (AUM) and fee revenue.

## 3. High-Stakes Negotiation with Prime Brokers
Senior leadership negotiates the commercial terms of the fund's relationship with prime brokers, including leverage ratios, margin requirements, and financing rates. These negotiations are critical for optimizing the fund's cost of capital and ensuring access to liquidity during periods of market stress.

# Essential Skills for Junior Hedge Fund Professionals

## 1. Advanced Excel and Financial Modeling
Proficiency in **Microsoft Excel**, including the ability to build complex, dynamic models without the use of a mouse, is a baseline requirement. Analysts must be capable of constructing DCF models, LBO models, and merger consequence analyses with 100% technical accuracy to support rapid investment decisions.

## 2. Quantitative Programming and Data Analysis
A working knowledge of **Python** or **R** is increasingly essential for modern hedge funds. Analysts must be able to use libraries like Pandas and NumPy to clean and analyze large data sets, allowing the fund to move beyond the limitations of traditional spreadsheet-based analysis.

## 3. Mastery of Accounting and Financial Statement Analysis
Analysts must possess a deep theoretical understanding of **GAAP** and **IFRS** to "normalize" earnings and identify aggressive accounting practices. This skill is vital for short-selling, where identifying deteriorating fundamentals or obscured liabilities is the primary source of alpha.

## 4. Professional Communication and Memo Writing
The ability to condense 50 hours of research into a 2-page investment memo is a core competency. Analysts must communicate a clear "thesis," "anti-thesis," and "catalyst" for every trade, ensuring that senior management can quickly grasp the risk-reward skew of a proposal.

## 5. Proficiency in Bloomberg and Market Data Systems
Expertise in navigating the **Bloomberg Terminal** is required for extracting real-time pricing, historical financials, and supply chain data. Analysts must know how to use "API" functions to pull this data directly into Excel or Python scripts for automated monitoring.
