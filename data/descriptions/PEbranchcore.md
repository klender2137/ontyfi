# Private Equity

**ID:** PE-001  
**Branch:** Private Equity

## Description

Private equity firms manage **more than $8 trillion in assets globally** and deploy hundreds of billions of dollars into companies every year. Typical deals range from **$50 million to several billion**, and firms usually hold investments for **3 to 7 years** before selling them. Top funds manage portfolios of **dozens of companies at once**, influencing thousands of employees and entire industries.

Private equity is the business of **buying companies, improving them, and selling them later for a profit**. Firms raise capital from investors such as pension funds, insurance companies, and wealthy families. They use that money to acquire companies, work with management to grow them, and exit through a sale or IPO.

* **Fundraising from Investors (Capital Raising)**
    * Private equity firms raise capital from institutional investors called Limited Partners.
    * These investors commit money for long periods, often 10 years.
    * The firm manages this capital through investment funds and charges management fees and a share of profits.
* **Deal Sourcing and Acquisition**
    * Investment teams search for companies that can grow or improve under new ownership.
    * They analyze industries, financial performance, and potential value creation.
    * If the opportunity looks strong, they structure a deal to acquire the company using equity and often borrowed money.
* **Financial Analysis and Deal Execution**
    * Before acquiring a company, teams build detailed financial models to estimate returns.
    * They perform due diligence on operations, accounting, legal risks, and market position.
    * If the investment case holds, they negotiate the purchase and complete the transaction.
* **Operational Improvement and Value Creation**
    * After the acquisition, the firm works closely with management to improve the company.
    * This may include expanding to new markets, reducing costs, or making add-on acquisitions.
    * The goal is to increase revenue, margins, and overall company value.
* **Exit Strategy and Investment Realization**
    * Private equity firms eventually sell their investments to realize profits.
    * Common exits include selling the company to a strategic buyer, another PE firm, or taking it public through an IPO.
    * Returns are then distributed to the investors and the fund managers.

### Entry Paths

* **The Investment Banking Analyst:** Typically holds a degree in finance, economics, or business. They possess elite skills in financial modeling, valuation, and deal analysis. Most work 2–3 years in investment banking before moving to PE as a prestigious "exit opportunity."
* **The Strategy Consultant:** Comes with a degree in business, economics, or engineering. They specialize in strategy analysis, market research, and operational improvement. They transition from corporate strategy projects into PE firms that focus heavily on operational value creation.
* **The Direct Undergraduate:** High-achieving students with degrees in finance or economics from top-tier universities. They demonstrate mastery in Excel modeling and accounting basics. A small number of these candidates are hired directly into analyst roles without prior full-time experience.
* **The Transaction Specialist:** Professionals with a background in Corporate Development or Big 4 accounting. They hold degrees in accounting or finance and are experts in transaction advisory and due diligence. They typically enter the industry by moving to mid-market PE firms.

### Market Breakdown

* **Leveraged Buyout (LBO) Funds:** These "classic" firms, like Blackstone or KKR, acquire mature, cash-flow-positive companies. They use significant debt to optimize operations and aim to exit at a much higher valuation.
* **Growth Equity Funds:** Firms like General Atlantic or Warburg Pincus target "proven" companies past the venture stage. They provide capital for geographic expansion or product development, usually taking minority stakes.
* **Distressed / Turnaround Funds:** Specialists like Apollo Global Management or Oaktree focus on "broken" companies. They buy debt at a discount or take control during bankruptcy to steer the company back to profitability.
* **Mezzanine Funds:** Firms such as Crescent Capital provide subordinated debt that sits between senior debt and equity. This "expensive" debt offers the lender equity-like warrants for extra upside potential.
* **Fund of Funds (FoF):** Rather than picking companies, firms like HarbourVest or Hamilton Lane pick the best PE managers. They allow smaller institutional investors to access "closed" elite funds through a diversified vehicle.

### Quick Summary

Private equity firms raise capital from large investors and use it to **buy, improve, and sell companies for profit**. The work combines **investing, financial analysis, and operational strategy**, which is why many professionals come from investment banking or consulting. What you see here is only the surface of the field, which includes dozens of strategies and career paths inside the global private markets ecosystem.
