# Tasks

**ID:** tasks
**Branch:** M&A / Investment Banking

## Description
## 1. Constructing Three-Statement Financial Models
Analysts utilize **Microsoft Excel** to build integrated financial models consisting of the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement. The objective is to project a company’s future financial performance based on historical data and growth assumptions. 

## 2. Performing Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Valuation
Using **Microsoft Excel**, analysts calculate the intrinsic value of a business by forecasting free cash flows and discounting them back to the present value using the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC). 

## 3. Spreading Trading Comparables (Comps)
Analysts extract financial data from **Bloomberg** or **FactSet** to calculate valuation multiples, such as EV/EBITDA and P/E ratios, for a peer group of publicly traded companies. 

## 4. Analyzing Precedent Transactions
Using databases like **Refinitiv** or **S&P Capital IQ**, analysts identify and summarize historical M&A deals within a specific industry. The output provides a "deal-based" valuation range.

## 5. Designing Pitch Books and Presentation Materials
Analysts utilize **Microsoft PowerPoint** to create comprehensive deal presentations that include market overviews, company profiles, and strategic rationale. 

## 6. Managing the Virtual Data Room (VDR)
Juniors coordinate with VDR providers such as **Intralinks** or **Datasite** to organize and upload sensitive corporate documents for due diligence. 

## 7. Compiling "Buyers Lists" and Market Maps
Analysts use **S&P Capital IQ** and industry reports to identify potential strategic and financial acquirers for a client’s business. 

## 8. Executing Accretion/Dilution Analysis
Using **Microsoft Excel**, analysts model the financial impact of a merger on the pro-forma earnings per share (EPS) of the acquiring company. 

## 9. Coordinating Due Diligence Workstreams
Analysts track information requests using **Microsoft Excel** or project management software to ensure all legal, financial, and operational queries from potential buyers are addressed.

## Strategic Tasks of Senior Investment Banking Professionals

## 1. Client Coverage and Relationship Origination
Managing Directors and Partners focus on maintaining long-term relationships with C-suite executives and board members of major corporations. Their primary objective is to identify future transaction needs and secure formal mandates for the firm.

## 2. High-Stakes Transaction Negotiation
Senior bankers lead the negotiation of definitive deal terms, including purchase price, indemnification structures, and closing conditions. They act as the lead intermediary between the buyer and seller to bridge valuation gaps and ensure deal certainty.

## 3. Strategic Board Advisory and Fairness Opinions
Directors and Managing Directors provide formal testimony to a company's board regarding the financial fairness of a proposed transaction. This involve strategic capital allocation advice and guidance on the long-term competitive positioning of the firm.

## Essential Skills for Investment Banking Analysts

## 1. Advanced Excel and Financial Modeling
Proficiency in **Microsoft Excel**, including the use of advanced formulas, macros, and short-cut keys, is a baseline requirement. Analysts must be able to build complex, error-free models quickly to support real-time deal discussions.

## 2. Mastery of Accounting and Financial Statement Analysis
A deep theoretical understanding of **Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP)** or **IFRS** is necessary. Analysts must be able to interpret financial statements to identify non-recurring items and normalize earnings for valuation purposes.

## 3. Proficiency in Financial Research Terminals
Expertise in navigating **Bloomberg**, **FactSet**, or **S&P Capital IQ** is essential for data extraction. Analysts must know how to efficiently pull historical financials, consensus estimates, and ownership data.

## 4. Technical Presentation and Pitch Writing
Success requires the ability to synthesize complex financial data into clear, concise **Microsoft PowerPoint** slides. This includes the creation of "waterfall" charts, "football field" valuation summaries, and professional-grade graphics.

## 5. Professional Communication and Memo Drafting
Analysts must be able to draft formal investment memos and internal briefing documents. This requires a high degree of precision in written communication to ensure that senior management and clients receive accurate and actionable information.
