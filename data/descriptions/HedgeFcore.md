## Hedge Funds

**ID:** Hfund-core
**Branch:** Hedge Funds
### Introduction

Over **$4.5 trillion in global assets under management** is currently controlled by hedge funds. The industry includes **more than 10,000 funds worldwide** employing sophisticated investment strategies across equities, bonds, currencies, commodities, and derivatives. Top hedge fund portfolio managers can earn **hundreds of millions of dollars annually**, while even junior analysts often earn **$150K–$600K total compensation** within a few years.

Hedge funds are investment firms that raise capital from institutional investors and wealthy individuals to invest in financial markets using advanced strategies. Unlike traditional asset managers that attempt to outperform a benchmark, hedge funds generally target **absolute returns**, meaning they aim to generate profits regardless of whether markets rise or fall. They actively trade liquid financial assets and frequently use leverage, short-selling, derivatives, and event-driven strategies to exploit market inefficiencies.

# Core Functions of the Hedge Fund Industry

### 1. Investment Idea Generation & Research

* Analysts constantly screen global markets for **mispriced assets** using valuation models, macroeconomic analysis, and sector research.
* Funds conduct **deep fundamental research** on companies, including financial statements, competitive positioning, and industry trends.
* Investment teams generate **trade theses and stock pitches**, recommending long or short positions based on expected price movements.

### 2. Portfolio Construction & Risk Allocation

* Portfolio managers design diversified portfolios where **position sizes reflect conviction levels and risk tolerance**.
* Advanced risk metrics such as **Value-at-Risk (VaR), exposure limits, and correlation analysis** help control downside risk.
* Capital is dynamically allocated between strategies such as equities, credit, macro trades, and arbitrage opportunities.

### 3. Trade Execution & Market Operations

* Traders implement investment ideas by **executing large transactions across exchanges and broker networks**.
* Execution strategies aim to **minimize transaction costs and market impact**, often using algorithmic trading systems.
* Hedge funds frequently employ **short-selling, derivatives contracts, and leverage** to amplify returns or hedge risk.

### 4. Event-Driven and Strategic Trading

* Many hedge funds exploit **corporate events** such as mergers, restructurings, spin-offs, bankruptcies, or regulatory changes.
* Analysts estimate **probabilities of deal completion** and price spreads to profit from merger arbitrage opportunities.
* Some funds pursue **activist strategies**, acquiring stakes in companies and influencing management decisions.

### 5. Investor Capital Management

* Hedge funds raise capital from **pension funds, endowments, sovereign wealth funds, and high-net-worth investors**.
* They charge a typical fee structure of **management fees (≈2% of assets)** plus **performance fees (≈20% of profits)**.
* Investor relations teams maintain transparency through **performance reporting, strategy explanations, and portfolio updates**.

# Entry Paths into the Hedge Fund Industry

### 1. Investment Banking Analyst Route
**Typical Degree:** Finance, Economics, or Business from a top university.
**Competencies:** Financial modeling, valuation, M&A analysis, company research.
**Entry Path:** Many hedge fund analysts previously worked **2–3 years in investment banking**, where they developed strong corporate valuation and deal analysis skills.

### 2. Equity Research Associate Route
**Typical Degree:** Finance, Accounting, or Economics.
**Competencies:** Financial statement analysis, industry research, earnings modeling, stock valuation.
**Entry Path:** Professionals working in **sell-side equity research at investment banks** transition to hedge funds because they already analyze public companies.

### 3. Sales & Trading / Markets Specialist Route
**Typical Degree:** Finance, Economics, or Mathematics.
**Competencies:** Market microstructure, derivatives pricing, trading strategies, macroeconomic analysis.
**Entry Path:** Traders from **rates desks, FX trading, or derivatives desks** join hedge funds that rely on macro or trading-intensive strategies.

### 4. Quantitative Analyst Route
**Typical Degree:** Mathematics, Physics, Computer Science, or Statistics.
**Competencies:** Python, machine learning, statistical modeling, algorithmic trading systems.
**Entry Path:** Quant hedge funds recruit candidates with strong **data science and quantitative modeling skills** to build trading algorithms and predictive models.

# Market Breakdown: Types of Hedge Fund Institutions

### Long/Short Equity Funds
These funds invest in stocks by simultaneously **buying undervalued companies (long positions) and short-selling overvalued ones**. Their core objective is to profit from relative differences between companies rather than overall market direction.
* **Viking Global Investors**
* **TCI Fund Management**
* **Marshall Wace**
* **Lone Pine Capital**
* **Egerton Capital**

### Global Macro Funds
Macro funds trade **currencies, commodities, bonds, and equity indices** based on economic trends such as interest rates, inflation, and geopolitical developments. They rely heavily on macroeconomic analysis and often use derivatives and leverage.
* **Bridgewater Associates**
* **Rokos Capital Management**
* **Brevan Howard Asset Management**
* **Tudor Investment Corporation**
* **Caxton Associates**

### Event-Driven Funds
Event-driven hedge funds focus on **corporate situations like mergers, restructurings, spin-offs, and bankruptcies**. Their strategies attempt to capture pricing inefficiencies created by complex corporate events.
* **Elliott Investment Management**
* **Farallon Capital Management**
* **Davidson Kempner Capital Management**
* **York Capital Management**
* **Third Point LLC**

### Quantitative Hedge Funds
Quant funds use **statistical models, machine learning, and algorithmic trading systems** to identify patterns in market data. Investment decisions are largely driven by automated models rather than discretionary analysis.
* **Renaissance Technologies**
* **Two Sigma Investments**
* **AQR Capital Management**
* **D.E. Shaw & Co.**
* **Arrowstreet Capital**

### Multi-Manager Platforms
These firms operate as **large investment platforms hosting multiple portfolio managers**, each running their own strategy within strict risk limits. The platform allocates capital across teams and continuously evaluates performance.
* **Millennium Management**
* **Citadel**
* **Point72 Asset Management**
* **Balyasny Asset Management (BAM)**
* **Schonfeld Strategic Advisors**

# Summary

Hedge funds are specialized investment firms that seek absolute returns by trading financial assets using sophisticated strategies such as short-selling, derivatives, and arbitrage. The industry attracts professionals with strong analytical skills from backgrounds like investment banking, equity research, trading, and quantitative science. Despite insane competition, hedge funds remain one of the most intellectually demanding and financially rewarding careers in global finance.
