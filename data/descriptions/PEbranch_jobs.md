# Private Equity Career

### Role Descriptions & Example Tasks

#### **1. Private Equity Analyst**
Analysts are usually recent graduates or former investment banking analysts. They support deals by performing heavy financial analysis and research.
* **Build an LBO model** to test whether a target company meets return requirements.
* **Research market size** and competitors for a potential acquisition target.
* **Prepare investment committee slides** summarizing financial projections and risks.

#### **2. Private Equity Associate (Pre-MBA)**
Associates are the core deal execution professionals. They work directly with senior staff to analyze acquisitions and structure investments.
* **Run detailed financial scenarios** in an LBO model to evaluate leverage levels and exit returns.
* **Coordinate due diligence** with consultants, lawyers, and accounting firms.
* **Draft sections of the investment memo** presented to the investment committee.

#### **3. Senior Associate**
Senior associates bring more deal experience and begin managing analysts and associates. They become more involved in portfolio monitoring.
* **Supervise analysts** building valuation models and verify assumptions used in projections.
* **Track operating performance** of portfolio companies and compare results against the investment case.
* **Prepare board materials** summarizing financial performance and strategic initiatives.

#### **4. Vice President (VP)**
Vice presidents lead deals from screening to execution and coordinate multiple teams working on the transaction.
* **Lead negotiations** on acquisition price and financing structure with bankers and lenders.
* **Manage due diligence** across legal, operational, and financial workstreams.
* **Present investment recommendations** directly to partners and investment committees.

#### **5. Managing Director / Partner**
Partners run the firm’s strategy, raise new funds, and originate large deals. They are responsible for long-term performance.
* **Secure capital commitments** from pension funds and institutional investors during fundraising.
* **Source new acquisition opportunities** through industry networks and investment banks.
* **Negotiate exit transactions** such as company sales or IPO processes.

## 2. Roles Across Different Private Equity Fund Types

Different private equity strategies require different work approaches. The following sections highlight responsibilities across the three core deal-team tiers.



### **I. Buyout Funds**
* **Analyst:** Build LBO models evaluating debt capacity; benchmark targets against competitors; track monthly portfolio performance.
* **Associate:** Coordinate full due diligence; structure financing with banks/private credit; prepare IC documentation.
* **Vice President:** Lead acquisition negotiations; design post-acquisition operational plans; manage exit processes.

### **II. Growth Equity Funds**
* **Analyst:** Analyze revenue metrics (ARR, CAC); research industry growth trends; prepare valuation comparisons.
* **Associate:** Evaluate founder teams and product strategy; structure minority investment terms; support follow-on rounds.
* **Vice President:** Negotiate terms with founders; develop international expansion strategies; prepare strategic acquisition exit options.

### **III. Venture Capital Funds**
* **Analyst:** Screen startup pitches for product-market fit; map emerging tech segments; track portfolio metrics.
* **Associate:** Conduct founder interviews; structure early-stage investment rounds/cap tables; support startup hiring.
* **Vice President:** Lead later-stage rounds; guide founders through scaling/fundraising; manage strategic acquirer discussions.

### **IV. Distressed / Special Situations Funds**
* **Analyst:** Analyze liquidity/debt for restructuring; model recovery values under bankruptcy; track creditor claims.
* **Associate:** Structure debt purchases or rescue financing; coordinate lender-management negotiations; analyze turnaround plans.
* **Vice President:** Lead restructuring negotiations with courts/creditors; design capital reorganizations; execute exits post-recovery.

### **V. Infrastructure Funds**
* **Analyst:** Model long-term cash flows for assets (airports, power plants); analyze regulatory frameworks; benchmark asset performance.
* **Associate:** Evaluate concession agreements; structure project finance/long-maturity debt; coordinate environmental diligence.
* **Vice President:** Negotiate asset acquisitions with governments; design infrastructure operational programs; lead long-term portfolio strategy.

## 3. Interview Preparation Insights

Private equity interviews focus on three theoretical concepts and two technical skill areas.

### **Theoretical Foundations**
1.  **LBO Theory:** Understanding how debt increases equity returns and how exit valuation drives investor $IRR$.
2.  **Valuation Methods:** Mastery of Comparable Company Analysis and Precedent Transactions.
3.  **Deal Structuring:** Understanding the interaction between equity ownership, debt financing, and investor protections.

### **Technical Skills**
* **Financial Modeling:** Proficiency in building LBO models in Excel, including multi-year projections and debt repayment schedules.
* **Statement Relationships:** Understanding how operating assumptions flow through the Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow Statement.

> **Note:** Interview discussions often culminate in a **Case Study** where the candidate must evaluate a real-world acquisition target.

## Summary
Private equity offers a highly structured career path combining financial analysis, high-stakes negotiation, and operational strategy. It attracts competitive individuals who enjoy complex deal-making and long-term value creation.
